Common Questions

What is a good financial health score?
70–100 is healthy. 45–69 needs attention. Under 45 means your finances are cooked and you need a plan immediately. The score weighs housing costs, debt, emergency fund, retirement contributions, savings rate, and budgeting habits.
Is my financial data private?
Yes. We never ask for your bank login, account numbers, or Social Security number. All quiz answers stay in your browser session only. We are not a financial institution and do not sell your data.
What percentage of income should go to savings?
Most financial experts recommend the 50/30/20 rule: 50% on needs, 30% on wants, and 20% on savings and debt repayment. A savings rate above 20% is considered strong. Below 10% is a warning sign, especially if you have no emergency fund.
How much should I have in my emergency fund?
3 to 6 months of living expenses is the standard. If you are self-employed or have variable income, aim for 6 months. Start with one month as your first goal — it prevents the debt spiral from unexpected expenses like car repairs or medical bills.
Should I pay off debt or invest first?
Always capture your employer 401k match first — it is a 100% instant return. Then pay off high-interest debt above 7% before investing. For low-interest debt like mortgages below 5%, investing in a diversified index fund like VOO or VTI historically outperforms paying it off early.
What countries does How Cooked Am I support?
US (401k, IRA, HSA, HYSA), UK (ISA, SIPP, workplace pension), Canada (RRSP, TFSA, FHSA), Australia (superannuation), Germany (ETF depot, Riester), France (Assurance-vie, PER, Livret A), India (EPF, PPF, NPS), Singapore (CPF, SSB, SRS), and UAE (zero income tax investing with IBKR or Saxo).
